**Goal:** Hire the Designer agent (6th and final pipeline role), wire MCP-isolated access to 21st.dev Magic + Figma from its heartbeats, amend `task-orchestration/SKILL.md` to spawn a Designer follow-up subtask when a slice has `needsDesignPolish: true`, and validate end-to-end on a small UI feature request.

**Architecture:** Two skill amendments plus one new agent plus one operator-side MCP config. `task-orchestration/SKILL.md` gains a new subsection (Stage 6 activation) that turns the existing `needsDesignPolish: boolean` read-only surface into an actual orchestration branch: when `true`, the Tech Lead creates a Designer follow-up subtask (`blockedByIssueIds: [<engineer-subtask>]`, `assigneeAgentId: null`, status `todo`) alongside the Engineer subtask for that slice, then progressively assigns it when the Engineer completes. `pipeline-dispatcher/SKILL.md` gains the concrete Designer routing rows (currently a placeholder). Validation reuses the throwaway `Paperclipowers Test` company (id `02de212f-0ec4-4440-ac2f-0eb58cb2b2ad`), both existing agents (`stage4-tech-lead`, `stage1-tester` Engineer; both paused at end of Stage 5 follow-up), plus `stage5-pm` and `stage5-reviewer`. Success is: Tech Lead creates an Engineer + Designer subtask pair; Engineer completes its slice; Designer wakes on the progressive-assignment PATCH with a fresh per-issue session; Designer uses the Magic + Figma MCP servers to produce a polish commit; final combined review passes.
